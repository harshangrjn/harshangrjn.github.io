Biography

I am currently a staff scientist in the “Applied Mathematics and Plasma Physics” group at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL). Previously, I was a post-doctoral research associate at Center for Nonlinear Studies (CNLS), LANL.

My primary research interests include development of efficient formulations and algorithms for modeling, design and control of complex physical systems. Particularly, I utilize tools from a myriad of disciplines including, mathematical programming and optimization (convex and nonconvex), control theory and uncertainty quantification. In recent years, at LANL, I have been focussing on various interesting problems arising in large-scale energy infrastructure systems. More recently, I have been working on application of discrete optimization methods in Quantum Computing. I was recently awarded with the “Outstanding Reviewer 2019” recognition from IEEE Transactions on Power Systems.

Software

  • Alpine: A Julia/JuMP-based novel global optimization solver for non-convex MINLPs.
    • Technical papers underlying Alpine: Paper 1, Paper 2, Paper 3, Poster
    • Presentation on Alpine at the 2nd Annual JuMP-dev Workshop, held at the Institut de Mathématiques de Bordeaux, FR, June 2018.

  • QuantumCircuitOpt: A Julia/JuMP-based package which implements discrete optimization-based methods for provably optimal synthesis of the architecture for Quantum circuits.

    • Technical paper underlying QCOpt: Paper 1
    • Presentation at the JuliaCon 2022, July 2022.
    • Presentation at the International Conference on Super Computing (SC21) for the Workshop on Quantum Computing Software, November 2021.
    • Presentation at the International Conference on Super Computing (SC22) for the Workshop on Quantum Computing Software, November 2022. Link

  • LaplacianOpt: A Julia/JuMP-based package which implements polyhedral relaxation-based algorithms for optimization of weighted graph Laplacians.

  • Juniper: A Julia/JuMP-based package which implements various nonlinear branch-and-bound algorithms to solve non-convex MINLPs to local optimality.

    • Technical paper underlying Juniper: Paper 1
